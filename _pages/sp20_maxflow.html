About this Group

In the past decade, major advancements in the design and analysis of algorithms have risen from a confluence of techniques developed in continuous and discrete optimization. One example in which this blend of approaches has been particularly effective is towards algorithms for solving maximum flow problems on graphs. This semester, we will read recent papers related to maxflow and learn about techniques developed in optimization related to analyzing Laplacian linear system solvers, interior point methods, and preconditioner construction.

Paper List

  • Electrical flows, laplacian systems, and faster approximation of maximum flow in undirected graphs
    • Paul Christiano, Jonathan A. Kelner, Aleksander Mądry, Daniel A. Spielman, and Shang-Hua Teng
  • Navigating central path with electrical flows: from flows to matchings, and back
    • Aleksander Mądry
  • Path finding methods for linear programming: Solving linear programs in \( \mathcal{O}(\sqrt{\text{rank}}) \) iterations and faster algorithms for maximum flows
    • Yin Tat Lee and Aaron Sidford
  • Computing maximum flows with augmenting electrical flows
    • Aleksander Mądry
  • Generalized preconditioning and network flow problems
    • Jonah Sherman
  • Flows in almost linear time via adaptive preconditioning
    • Rasmus Kyng, Richard Peng, Sushant Sachdeva, Di Wang
  • Faster energy maximization for faster maximum flows
    • Yang P. Liu and Aaron Sidford
